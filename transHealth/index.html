Gender Affirming Care

As defined by the World Health Organization gender affirming health care is 'any single or combination of a number of social, psychological, behavioural or medical (including hormonal treatment or surgery) interventions to support and affirm an individuals's gender identity.'


Benefits

Many children experience 'Gender Dysphoria' and feel a distinct disconnect between their assigned gender at birth and their gender identity. Countless studies have shown that these children are much more likely to experience bullying and other forms of violence. Because of this many transgender youth experience emotion distress and depression and many are driven to self-harm and suicide. Gender affirming healthcare can greatly mitigate the chance of suicide among transgender children. In a study of more than 9000 transgender and non-binary youths they concluded that trans and non-binary youth under the age of 18 are nearly 40 percent less likely to experience depression and attempt suicide


Opposition

Despite the clear benefits many people oppose trans healthcare because they do not believe that trans people exist or that they are somehow sinful for wanting to affirm their gender identity. These people have significant power and are pushing to ban gender-affirming care in their countries and states. If they get their way the suicide rates of the trans community will undoubtedly rise and the stigma they face on a daily basis will be further reinforced.

Climate Change

Climate change refers to long-term shifts in temperatures and weather patterns. These can be natural, but since the 1800s humans have been the main cause for climate change by burning fossil fuels like coal, oil and gas. When these fossil fuels are burned it creates greenhouse gasses which make their way into the atmosphere and trap the sun's heat.


C02 and Methane

The two main greenhouse gasses responsible for climate change are carbon-dioxide and methane. These gasses are produced from gasoline when driving a car, or coal when heating a building. Almost every daily task that requires electricity contributes to greenhouse gas emissions because of the reliance on fossil fuels for generating electricity around the world.


Humans are the problem

We have the solution

Despite the daunting problem, we know how to stop it. We all know that we need to reduce emissions and a large number of people are trying to reduce their individual carbon footprint. However, even if everyone in the world cuts down on their personal emissions we still won't be out of the fire, A large proportion of the world's emissions are from large companies that benifit financially from using fossil fuels instead of more expensive cleaner forms of power. If these companies face enough pressure from the public and from governments they will be forced to cut down on their emissions by more meaningful amounts than they have promised to do thus far.

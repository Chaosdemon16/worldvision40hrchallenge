Xenophobia

Xenophobia is the 'fear and contempt of strangers or foreigners or of anything designated as foreign or a conviction that certain foreign individuals and cultures represent a threat to the authentic identity of one's own nation-state and cannot integrate into the local society peacefully'. Essentially, Xenophobia is the contempt or fear of foreigners integrating into society.


Xenophobia in South Africa

In the particularly extreme example of South Africa you can see what the implications that Xenophobia can have are more than just snide comments. According to WitWatersrand University in South Africa's Xenowatch xenophobic attacks resulted in a total of 669 deaths, 5,310 looted shops, and 127,572 displacements between 1994 and March 2024. In May of 2008 alone attacks took place in at least 135 different locations aroud the country. These attacks did not target white people but targeted migrants from other african countries and from south asian countries who they blamed for increased crime and unemployment.

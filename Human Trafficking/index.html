Human Trafficking Prevention

What is human trafficking?

Human Trafficking is the unlawful act of transporting or coercing people in order to benefit from their work or service, typically in the form of forced labour or sexual exploitation.

Statistics

  • Human Traffickers earn $150B in profits every year
  • It is estimated that over 40 million people become victims of human trafficking every year
  • Over 50% of cases are sex trafficking involving minors

Most Common Forms of human trafficking

  • Sex Trafficking 38.7%
  • Forced labour 38.8%
  • Begging, organ removal and other purposes 11.4%
  • Forced criminal activity 10.2%
  • Forced marriges 0.9%

Data Privacy

The Privacy Act 2020

Your Rights

  • You know when and why your information is being collected
  • Your information is used and shared apropriately
  • Your information is kept safe and secure
  • You can access the information an organisation holds about you

Correcting your information

If you think an organisation holds incorrect information about you, you can ask them to correct it. If they decline, They must provide a statement of correction and a reason why on request. If they refuse to provide a statement of correction you can make a complaint to the privacy commissioner

Steps to resolving privacy issues

  1. Contact the organisation
  2. Allow 20 working days for a response

    3. If you don't recive an adequate response

  3. Make a complaint to the privacy commissioner

How complaints work

If you can't work out your issue with the organisation, then you can complain to the privacy commissioner

Reasons they won't investigate

  • You haven't contacted the organisation first
  • Your complaint is about an old issue
  • The issue hasn't caused you harm
  • The agency has taken appropriate steps to resolve the issue

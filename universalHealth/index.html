Universal Healthcare

Universal Healthcare also known as Universal Health Coverage means that all people have access to the full range of quality health services they need - wherever they may be and without financial hardship.


United States

Despite the United States spending much more on healthcare than other countries per capita the quality of its healthcare is much less. To put it in perspective the US spends almost twice as much per capita on healthcare but has the lowest life expectancy among large wealthy countries. The reason that the United States healthcare system is so bad despite so much funding is because it does not universal healthcare coverage. Instead, Americans have to pay for healthcare from their pocket, or get health insurance. The government does subsides some things though, elderly, people with disabilities, veterans, and low-income people. Even with subsides healthcare prices are still very high so most of the population has health insurance, either by paying themselves or from employee benefits. There is still 8.5% of the population that is not insured and therefore need to pay sky-high healthcare costs if something should arise


New Zealand

New Zealand is among the top ten healthcare systems in the world, mostly because of universal healthcare coverage. In New Zealand citizens and permanent residents get free or low-cost healthcare with the government paying for most of it. Hospitals and specialist care are covered by the government if a patient is reffered by a GP (General Practitioner). Seeing a GP can cost anywhere between $19 and $55 but those at a lower income can apply for a community services card which will reduce the already low cost of presriptions and GP visits.

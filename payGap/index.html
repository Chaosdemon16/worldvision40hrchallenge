The Gender Pay Gap

The gender pay gap is the difference between women's and men's average hourly earnings. The current country-wide rate is 8.6% which means that on average women are paid 8.6% less than men this rate is even less for MƒÅori, Pacific, Asian, and Disabled Women.


What causes it

We don't know for sure how much the gender pay gap is influenced by certain things, but we do know some things that contribute to the gender pay gap. Many people say that the gender pay gap is caused by the differences in occupation and industries (women are more likely to go into lower-paid industries) but that only makes up about 20% of the current gender pay gap. We know this because within industries there is still a large gender pay gap. What is likely the largest cause of the gender pay gap is the concious and unconcious biases of employers. An employer is more likely to higher a man for a high paying job because of sexism - perhaps not conciously.


How to lower it

Because there are so many causes of the gender pay gap it is hard to find a solution. Pay gaps can be reduced with pay equity (the same pay for the same amount of work) and employers simply hiring more women.

Make it 16

Many people are campaigning to lower the voting age in New Zealand to 16 from 18. Their reasoning is that 16 and 17-year-olds are just as impacted by government decisions as people 18 and over and should therefore have a say in those decisions. Their arguments align with the supreme court's ruling that preventing 16 and 17-year-olds from voting was unjustified age discrimination and that it was in breach of the bill of rights. In early 2023 the labour government introduced a bill to lower the voting age to 16 and it passed the first reading.

Deep sea mining

Deep-sea mining is the process of extracting mineral deposits from the ocean floor at depths of more than 200 meters. These deposits can be found in polymetallic nodules, polymetallic sulphides, and metal-rich crusts on underwater mountains. The minerals are essential for many things, including wind turbines, solar panels, electric vehicles, batteries, and zero-carbon energy components.

The Problem with Deep Sea Mining

  • Destructive processes
  • Radioactive exposure
  • Creating weapons for war

Alternatives to Deep Sea Mining

  • Landfill mining
  • Battery recycling

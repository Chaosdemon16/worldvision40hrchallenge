HIV

HIV (human immunodeficiency virus) is a virus that attacks the cells that help the body to fight infection, which makes a person more vulnerable to other infections and diseases. It is spread through direct contact certain bodily fluids of a person with HIV. Most commonly through unprotected sex or through the sharing of injection drug equipment. If untreated, HIV can lead to the disease AIDS (acquired immunodeficiency syndrome)


AIDS

AIDS is the latest stage of HIV infection and only occurs when the body's immune system is so badly damaged from HIV. In developed countries most people with HIV don't develop AIDS because they take HIV medicine which stops the progression of the disease. After developing AIDS the person will typically survive around 3 years, less if they are infected with another disease. Because the odds of surviving AIDS are so small, it is important to be tested for HIV in the earlier stages.


Treatment

HIV cannot be cured, but it can be prevented from developing into AIDS through HIV medecine (called antiretroviral therapy). If taken as prescribed HIV medicine will reduce the the amount of HIV in the blood to a very low level. People taking HIV medicine can live long and healthy lives and will not transmit HIV through sex.


Statistics

Since the start of the epedemic in 1981 85.6 million people have becom infected with HIV and 40.4 million people have died from AIDS related illnesses. At the end of 2022 39 million people were living with HIV with 1.3 million people newly infected that year.

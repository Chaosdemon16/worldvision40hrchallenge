Clean Energy

Over the past few decades the world has begun to transition to clean energy from fossil fuels. Electric vehicles are in demand and the world is using more and more batteries. This increase in demand is hand-in-hand with an increase in demand for lithium to create these resources.


Environmental Impacts of Lithium Mining

Lithium mining results in about 15 Tonnes of CO2 emissions and an estimated 2.2 million litres of water to mine 1 tonne of lithuim. The C02 emissions worsen the effects of climate change and the significant water use leads to scarcity in already arid regions. When you consider that 130,00 tonnes of lithium are produced every year you start to realise the sheer magnitude of environmental destruction that is caused by lithium mining. Not only that but the chemicals used in lithium mining can contaminate soil and water sources which poses risks to human health and wildlife.

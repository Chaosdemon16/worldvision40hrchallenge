Human Rights

Human rights are inherent to every human being. They apply regardless or race, sex, nationality, ethnicity, language, religion, sexual orientation, or any other status. Every human is entitled to human rights without discrimination as outlined in the Universal Declaration of Human Rights. The Universal Declaration of Human Rights lays out the rights of every human including the right of life and liberty, freedom from slavery and torture, freedom of opinion and expression, the right to work and education and many more.


Human Rights Council

The UN Human Rights Council is the key intergovernmental body responsible for ensuring that human rights are upheld around the globe by addressing any situations in which human rights have been violated. It is made up of 47 State Representatives from different countries.

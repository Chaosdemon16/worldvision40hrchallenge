Department of Conservation

The Department of Conservation is the government body in charge of the conservation of New Zealand's natural environment. DOC controls and manages about 8 million hectares of native bush, marine reserves and wetlands, as well as many offshore islands. It is responsible for pest control and the maintaining of tracks and huts in all national parks. All backcountry recreation is provided for and managed by DOC.


Trapping

DOC plays a leading role in reaching the goal of Predator Free 2050 as it controls over 30% of the total land area of New Zealand. It has a robust trapping network to catch pests in national parks and provides many resources for people to start backyard trapping.
